Abstract MediCareIQ is a digital healthcare platform in Nepal that combines advanced technology with medical expertise to provide seamless telemedicine services.  Users can easily register, login, and choose from a variety of healthcare providers, including physicians and specialists. The platform also offers appointment booking, streamlined payment processing, and secure video consultations.  MediCareIQ uses machine learning algorithms to analyze medical reports, enabling accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment recommendations. It also serves as a comprehensive health hub, offering health resources, articles, videos, and interactive tools for self-care and health education. The platform is built on a robust technological framework, with a user-friendly frontend in ReactJS and a reliable backend in NodeJS and MySQL. It prioritizes security and privacy, ensuring user data and confidentiality. MediCareIQ is a transformative force in Nepal's healthcare landscape, empowering individuals to take control of their health and well-being. Ensuring good health is crucial in today's fast-paced world, but it's not always easy. In Nepal,

MediCareIQ caters to two types of patients: those who require regular check-ups and those who

scheduling appointments with physicians, discussing symptoms, and receiving recommended tests 
and prescriptions directly to their email addresses. Once they have obtained the recommended 
tests, patients can locate appropriate test centers for conducting the tests. After receiving the test 
reports, the physician reviews them and provides advice, such as prescribing medications or 
referring the patient to a specialist doctor for further evaluation. Payment is a one-time payment 
that covers appointments with both physicians and specialists.

  3 1.3 Problem Domain  
1. Unequal Access to Healthcare Resources and Predominance of Rural Population:  
Nepal's healthcare system is significantly disadvantaged, with one doctor for every 850 people in 
the Kathmandu Valley and only one doctor for every 150,000 people in remote rural areas with 
78.55 percent of Nepal's population being rural region. (Singh, 2023) This disparity, exacerbated 
by geographical remoteness, poor infrastructure, and a shortage of healthcare professionals, results 
in limited medical assistance for rural populations, leading to worsened health outcomes and 
increased mortality rates. 
 
2. Difficulty Finding Suitable Health Centers and Economic Barriers to Healthcare:  
Nepal's healthcare system faces challenges in locating suitable facilities, particularly in rural areas, 
due to a lack of knowledge, reliable health information, and economic constraints. Remote regions 
often lack the knowledge to identify nearby healthcare centers, making it difficult for people to 
understand necessary tests or treatments. Because 25.2% of the Nepali population lives below the 
national poverty line, the costs of commuting to urban areas and possible income loss during illness 
discourage many from obtaining timely medical care. (Neupane P, et al., 2021) 
 
3. Late Diagnosis, Preventable Deaths, and Lack of Health Information:  
Late diagnosis of medical conditions in Nepal is a significant issue mainly due to limited access 
to healthcare, low awareness levels about health issues, and financial constraints. This leads to 
advanced diseases, unnecessary suffering, and preventable deaths. The lack of health information 
further complicates the issue, as individuals struggle to make informed decisions about their 
healthcare needs. In 2017, the death rate from diarrheal diseases in Nepal was 36.1 per 100,000 
population, largely due to a lack of health information, poor awareness, and insufficient preventive 
efforts. (Nepal Health Research Council (NHRC), Ministry of Health and Population (MoHP) and 
Monitoring Evaluation and Operational Research (MEOR)., 2019)

Unit testing and system testing are crucial in the software development lifecycle, ensuring the 
quality and reliability of the final product. 
Unit testing tests individual components or units of code in isolation, identifying and fixing defects 
early in the process. However, it may overlook integration issues and interactions between 
different units, highlighting the need for comprehensive system testing.  
System testing evaluates the entire system's behaviour, validating its compliance with functional 
and non-functional requirements. It includes functional testing, usability testing, performance 
testing, and security testing. Although it provides a broader scope of coverage, it may be more 
complex to set up and execute. 
 Both tests have strengths and limitations, with unit testing excelling at detecting and fixing defects 
at the code level and facilitating rapid feedback cycles, while system testing provides a 
comprehensive evaluation of the entire system's functionality and performance. A balanced 
approach combining both is essential for delivering a high-quality, reliable software product like 
MediCareIQ. (Point, 2024)

5.1.1 Legal Issues 
1. Compliance with National Healthcare Regulations 
MediCareIQ is required to comply with national healthcare regulations, which entail ensuring that 
healthcare providers are licensed and qualified, adhering to service scope and limitations, and 
understanding the regulations governing patient-provider interaction. The platform must also 
follow clinical practice guidelines established by the Nepal Medical Council or other health 
organizations to ensure that patient care meets nationally accepted standards. If MediCareIQ is 
required to register as a telemedicine service provider, it may be necessary to provide detailed 
documentation and undergo review by health authorities to ensure the platform's safety, reliability, 
and benefits for patients. (Nepal Health Research Council (NHRC), Ministry of Health and 
Population (MoHP) and Monitoring Evaluation and Operational Research (MEOR)., 2019) 
 
2. Data Protection and Privacy Laws 
Under the Information Technology Act, MediCareIQ is required to comply with strict data 
protection and privacy laws to ensure the confidentiality of patient information. To achieve this, 
the platform must implement robust security measures such as encryption, database security, and 
legal compliance. Additionally, it must establish a clear data retention policy and provide 
transparent privacy policies. Users must be informed about data collection, usage, and sharing, and 
they have the right to access and request data deletion. In the event of data breaches, MediCareIQ 
must respond promptly to demonstrate its commitment to responsible data management and to 
maintain trust in its services. (Pradhan, 2024) 
 
3. Telemedicine Practice Guidelines 
Telemedicine practice guidelines are crucial in Nepal for telemedicine platforms like MediCareIQ 
to maintain high standards of medical practice and ensure patient safety. These guidelines 
encompass operational and clinical aspects, including patient privacy, consent procedures, and 
technical requirements for remote consultations. MediCareIQ must comply with these guidelines

and determining appropriate telemedicine conditions. Additionally, they mandate detailed record-
keeping practices for continuity of care, auditing, and accountability. By adhering to these 
guidelines, MediCareIQ can instill trust among its users and provide safe, efficient, and effective 
healthcare services. (Nepal Health Research Council (NHRC), Ministry of Health and Population 
(MoHP) and Monitoring Evaluation and Operational Research (MEOR)., 2019) 
 
4. Other legal issues 
MediCareIQ has been assigned the responsibility of ensuring compliance with various legal 
standards. It is required to protect intellectual property and abide by national regulations for 
medical advertising, preventing misinformation, and maintaining service integrity. The platform 
must also comply with electronic transactions laws to ensure transparency and user-friendliness. 
Furthermore, MediCareIQ must ensure that all healthcare providers are appropriately credentialed 
and adhere to prescribed medical standards. Compliance with Nepal's legal framework necessitates 
a comprehensive approach that encompasses data protection, healthcare regulation, intellectual 
property rights, and e-commerce laws. (Pradhan, 2024)

The successful implementation of MediCareIQ in Nepal is essential due to the digital divide, which 
represents a gap in technology infrastructure, as well as the skills required to effectively engage 
with digital tools. To overcome this challenge, the platform must address both hardware limitations 
and software knowledge and comfort levels of its users. To achieve this, the platform should 
initiate grassroots-level training programs, in collaboration with local health posts, community 
centers, and schools, aimed at educating the population on digital navigation, online health 
information literacy, and using MediCareIQ. Moreover, the platform should be accessible on low-
specification devices and have an intuitive interface, with voice-navigation systems in multiple 
languages and culturally recognizable visual icons. These targeted strategies aim to bridge the 
digital divide and make digital healthcare a new norm in Nepal. (Human Rights Watch, 2024)

Stigma associated with certain diseases, such as HIV/AIDS, mental health disorders, tuberculosis, 
and sexually transmitted diseases, can discourage individuals from seeking medical help. In Nepal, 
MediCareIQ must handle disease-related stigma by ensuring anonymity and confidentiality. 
Maintaining strict data privacy is crucial for protecting user information and building trust in 
digital health platforms. In Nepal, data protection laws are still developing, and public awareness 
about digital rights is limited. Therefore, MediCareIQ needs to implement robust encryption 
methods, secure data storage solutions, and clear privacy policies to safeguard user data. Educating 
users about their data rights can help build trust and empower them to take control of their health 
data. (Human Rights Watch, 2024)

opportunities and challenges. AI can enhance efficiency and diagnostic accuracy, but it can also 
depersonalize care and marginalize human medical expertise. To address this, MediCareIQ should 
focus on augmenting the skills of healthcare professionals and ensuring technology remains a 
supportive tool. The platform should also consider economic barriers, implementing tiered pricing

1. Data Privacy and Patient Confidentiality 
 
MediCareIQ is addressing the ethical obligation of upholding data privacy and patient 
confidentiality. The platform's sensitive nature, including personal identifiers and medical details, 
requires stringent protective measures. Threats include cyber-attacks, inadvertent leaks, and 
mishandling. To address these challenges, MediCareIQ can use multiple layers of solutions, 
including end-to -end encryption, access controls based on least privilege, and regular audits. It can 
also provide comprehensive training to staff on privacy and ethical handling of patient information. 
Transparent policies are communicated to users, and the platform evolves with advanced 
cybersecurity measures. This commitment to data protection positions MediCareIQ as a standard-
bearer for ethical digital healthcare in Nepal. (Pradhan, 2024) 
 
2. AI Deployment and Medical Accuracy 
MediCareIQ's AI deployment in digital healthcare raises ethical concerns about medical accuracy 
and the reliability of AI-driven diagnoses. The platform's AI algorithms process medical reports 
and predict illnesses, but they can propagate systemic biases, especially in Nepal's diverse 
population. The platform relies on data imbalanced by demographics, socioeconomic status, and 
regional health trends, potentially leading to biased algorithms. Misdiagnosis by AI can result in 
improper treatment, patient distress, and eroded trust in the healthcare system. To address these 
challenges, MediCareIQ should prioritize transparency, accountability, and continuous learning 
within its AI systems. This approach, along with ethical AI principles, can strengthen trust in the 
platform and enhance the accuracy and dependability of medical care. 
 
3. Other Ethical Issues  
MediCareIQ is facing an ethical dilemma in terms of perpetuating healthcare inequalities in Nepal. 
The platform's accessibility may be limited to urban users with stable internet connectivity, while 
rural or impoverished regions may be excluded due to lack of access. Therefore, the inclusivity of 
the platform must be continually scrutinized to ensure that it serves all groups of people, including 
the elderly, less educated, disabled, and those who speak minority languages.

learning algorithms. 
5. Health Information Dissemination: Users can access health-related content such as articles, 
videos, and other resources. 
6. Secure Payment Mechanism: This feature facilitates secure and affordable payments for 
telemedicine consultations.

  274 7.4.4 Considered Methodologies  
The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is an architecture that outlines the procedures 
that must be followed during each stage of software development. It includes a thorough outline 
of the software's development, deployment, and maintenance strategy. The SDLC describes the 
complete development cycle by outlining the numerous steps needed to create high-quality 
software products. The stages of the SDLC encompass the entire life cycle of the software, from 
the initial phase to the deployment of the product, in a methodical and organized manner. 
Requirements gathering, designing, coding, testing, and maintenance are the various phases 
involved in the SDLC. (Tutorials Point, 2022) 
 
Figure 288: Software Development Lifecycle  
 
There are various software development methodologies available according to the requirements 
and goals of the project, and each process model adheres to a set of stages that are exclusive to 
its kind.

  275 7.4.4.1 Waterfall Methodology  
The waterfall model (also known as the linear-sequential life cycle model) is a traditional 
software development model that uses a linear and sequential technique for constructing the 
system. The model is called a waterfall because it progresses methodically from one phase to the 
next logically and sequentially. The outcome of one phase is used as the input for the following 
phase in this paradigm, which is separated into multiple phases. There is no overlap between 
phases, and each one must be completed before the next one begins. (JavaTpoint, 2023) 
 
 
Figure 289: Waterfall Methodology

  276 7.4.4.2 Agile Methodology   
Agile Methodology is a software development method based on iterative development in which 
the task is divided into smaller iterations or parts. The project's requirements and scope are 
established at the initial phase of the development process, and plans for the number of iterations, 
their duration, and their scope are made beforehand. In the Agile process model, each iteration is 
regarded as a brief time "frame" that normally lasts one to four weeks  The project risk and entire 
project delivery time requirements are minimized by dividing the complete project into smaller 
sections. Before a working product is presented to the client, each iteration requires a team to go 
through the entire software development life cycle, including planning, requirements analysis, 
design, coding, and testing. (JavaTpoint, 2023)

The Prototype model is a software development method in which the prototype is constructed 
initially with the minimum requirements. After that, the prototype is tested and adjusted in 
response to the client's feedback until a final prototype with the required features is constructed. 
The final prototype serves as a foundation for the finished product. It is an iterative, trial-and-error 
process that occurs between the developer and the client. (JavaTpoint, 2023)
